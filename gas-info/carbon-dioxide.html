Carbon Monoxide (CO)

What is Carbon Monoxide?

Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas that is toxic to humans and animals. It is produced by incomplete combustion of carbon-containing fuels.

Sources

  • Vehicle exhaust
  • Gas stoves and heaters
  • Fireplaces and furnaces
  • Industrial processes

Health Effects

  • Headaches and dizziness
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Chest pain
  • Confusion and disorientation
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Death in severe cases

Safety Thresholds

  • 0-50 ppm: Safe level
  • 50-100 ppm: Mild symptoms
  • 100+ ppm: Dangerous level
  • 400+ ppm: Life-threatening
