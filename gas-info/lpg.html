LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas)

What is LPG?

LPG is a mixture of propane and butane that is stored as a liquid under pressure. It's commonly used as a fuel for cooking, heating, and vehicles.

Sources

  • Gas cylinders
  • Storage tanks
  • Gas pipelines
  • Industrial facilities

Health Effects

  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Breathing difficulties
  • Unconsciousness
  • Frostbite (liquid form)

Safety Thresholds

  • 0-500 ppm: Safe level
  • 500-1000 ppm: Warning level
  • 1000+ ppm: Dangerous level
  • 2000+ ppm: Explosive risk
